The Evolution of Print

From ancient craft to modern mastery

200 AD

Woodblock Printing

The earliest known form of printing. Artisans carved entire pages into wooden blocks, inked them, and pressed them onto cloth and paper. Each block was a work of art in itself.

1440

The Gutenberg Press

Johannes Gutenberg's movable type revolutionized civilization. For the first time, knowledge could be reproduced at scale. The printed word changed the world forever.

1800s

The Letterpress

Raised metal type pressed directly into paper, leaving a distinctive impression you could feel with your fingertips. The craft of print became tactile art.

1900s

Offset Lithography

The image transfers from plate to rubber blanket to paper. Cleaner, faster, more precise. Print became accessible to every business and household.

Today

Digital Precision

Modern print combines centuries of knowledge with digital exactness. Vibrant color, razor-sharp detail, and infinite possibility on every substrate imaginable.
